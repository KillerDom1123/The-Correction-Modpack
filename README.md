## Overview

Nobody knows exactly what happened.

The world you inherit is the scarred remnant of something far greater. Abandoned machines still wait for purpose. Ancient knowledge lies scattered across forgotten places. Strange creatures roam lands that should be empty. Those who came before are remembered only as **the Old Civilisation** — some say engineers, some say scholars of magic, some believe they discovered something that neither science nor magic could explain.

Generations have passed since the collapse. Their descendants have begun rebuilding: small settlements take root, forgotten crafts return, lost technologies flicker back to life. But the wilderness is no longer simply *unexplored* — it is **unknown**. Deep beneath the earth and in the dark between the trees, things move that do not behave like anything found in nature.

Before the end, the Old Civilisation reached further than ever before — building machines to travel beyond their world, searching for something. The records stop before the truth is known.

Now you walk their ruins, inheriting their machines, their magic, and their mistakes. The ruins remain. The signals continue. The darkness waits.

**What happened after The Correction?**

## What kind of pack is this?

A slow, dangerous, atmosphere-first experience — **not** a kitchen-sink power fantasy. It blends four things and asks you to earn all of them:

- **Survival** against a hostile world — cold, darkness, hunger, and things that watch.
- **Rebuilding civilisation** from fragments.
- **Earned progression** through technology *and* magic, gated so power feels achieved, not handed out.
- **Exploration** as the heart of the game — the answers are out there, not in your crafting grid.

If you enjoy tension, gradual mastery, and a world that tells its story through what it left behind, this is for you. If you want instant flight and infinite resources by day three, this is not.

## Core Features

- **A world that fights back.** Temperature and climate matter (freezing nights, dangerous weather, harsh biomes), darkness is genuinely dark, and shelter, light, and warmth are early priorities.
- **Rebuild society.** Grow a colony from a handful of survivors into a living settlement — one that needs walls, guards, and lighting to endure the night.
- **Earn your technology.** A deliberate progression curve — from simple mechanical automation, through industrial power and advanced processing, to digital storage and autocrafting. Each tier is a real project, not a quick recipe.
- **A parallel path of magic.** Rituals, spirits, glyphs, and arcane research stand alongside the machines as a different way of understanding reality.
- **Rare, psychological horror.** The frights here are uncommon by design — dread and the unexpected, not nightly swarms. A single encounter should be a story you tell later.
- **Exploration-driven.** Structures, ruins, and rare biomes hide the world's history; rail networks and waystones connect what you rebuild.
- **A distant final chapter.** Beyond the world lies the Old Civilisation's unfinished journey — reachable only once you've truly rebuilt what they had.

## The Four Paths

The mystery can be approached from any direction. Every major system exists for a narrative reason:

| Path | Question it asks | Mods |
|---|---|---|
| **Engineering** | *What did they build?* | Create (+ addons), Immersive Engineering, PneumaticCraft, Mekanism |
| **Arcane Studies** | *What did they understand?* | Ars Nouveau, Occultism, Hexerei, Forbidden Arcanus, Iron's Spells |
| **The Archive** | *What did they hide?* | Applied Energistics 2, CC:Tweaked |
| **Horror Investigation** | *What survived?* | The horror suite — encountered, never "farmed" |
| **The Descendants** | *Can it be rebuilt?* | MineColonies |
| **The Final Journey** | *Where did they go?* | Ad Astra |

Each path is independently playable. You don't have to follow all of them — but the more you explore, the more the world reveals.

## What to Expect

- **Progression is intentional and slow.** Ore multiplication, autocrafting, flight, and space are gated behind real infrastructure. You will rebuild civilisation the long way.
- **Resources and rewards are scarce.** Exploration is rewarded with *knowledge and progress* more than with overpowered gear.
- **The quest book is an investigation framework, not a checklist.** It offers direction and teaches the mods when you ask "what now?" — but you can ignore half of it and still play your own way.
- **Minimal hand-holding.** The world expects you to leave your base, take risks, and piece things together yourself.

## Getting Started

No spoilers — just footing:

1. **Survive the first nights.** Prioritise shelter, light, warmth, and food before anything ambitious. The cold and the dark are threats in their own right.
2. **Don't rush the tech tree.** Early machines and a stable base beat sprinting toward late-game gear you can't sustain.
3. **Leave home.** The pack rewards exploration — ruins and structures are where the world opens up.
4. **Use the quest book for direction, not obligation.** It points you at the major systems when you're unsure what to do next.
5. **Pay attention.** The world documents itself. What you find matters more than it first appears.

## Multiplayer / Dedicated Server

The pack is **server-ready**. To host:

1. Install a matching **Forge 1.20.1 (47.4.10)** dedicated server with the **same mod list and versions** as clients.
2. Copy these directories to the server: **`mods/`**, **`config/`** (includes the OpenLoader datapacks and quests), **`defaultconfigs/`**, and **`kubejs/`**. Copy `saves/<world>` → the server's `world/` if migrating an existing map.
3. Keep `mods/` and `kubejs/startup_scripts/` **identical** on server and clients (registry parity), or players will be unable to connect.

Client-only tweaks (audio, video, personal options) stay on each player's client and are ignored by the server.

## Repository & Configuration

This repository tracks the pack's **configuration and custom content**, not the mod binaries:

- **Tracked:** `config/` (all mod configs, OpenLoader datapacks, FTB Quests), `kubejs/` (custom scripts/items), `defaultconfigs/` (per-world defaults), and the `docs/` markdown.
- **Not tracked:** `mods/`, `saves/`, caches, logs, and machine-specific files (see `.gitignore`).

The pack is heavily **rebalanced by design** — see **[docs/CONFIG_CHANGES.md](docs/CONFIG_CHANGES.md)** for a full per-mod record of every configuration change and the reasoning behind it (progression gating, horror rarity, climate, colony danger, etc.).

Because `mods/` isn't tracked, use **[modlist.md](modlist.md)** as the authoritative record of which mods and versions the configuration targets.
