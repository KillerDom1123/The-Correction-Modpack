# **The World After The Correction**

> *"The Old Civilisation did not fall because they lacked knowledge.*
>
> *They fell because they found something they could not understand."*

Nobody knows exactly what happened.

The world you have inherited is a scarred remnant of something far greater. Abandoned machines still wait for purpose. Ancient knowledge lies scattered across forgotten places. Strange creatures roam lands that should be empty.

Those who came before are remembered only as **the Old Civilisation**.

Some remember them as engineers.
Some remember them as scholars of magic.
Some believe they discovered something that neither science nor magic could explain.

The truth has been buried beneath broken inventions, lost records, and memories that refuse to disappear.

---

# **A World Rebuilt From Fragments**

Generations have passed since the collapse.

The descendants of the Old Civilisation have begun rebuilding their world. Small settlements are growing again. Forgotten crafts are being rediscovered. Lost technologies are slowly returning.

The engineers of the Old Civilisation left behind machines far beyond the understanding of those who followed.

Their mechanical systems could automate entire industries. Their factories could transform raw materials into impossible creations. Their computers stored knowledge that could survive even when the people who created it were gone.

Through inventions like mechanical automation, advanced energy systems, artificial intelligence, and digital storage, they sought to remove every limitation placed upon them.

But engineering was only one path.

The magical scholars of the Old Civilisation pursued a different understanding of reality.

Through rituals, spirits, ancient texts, and arcane experimentation, they searched for forces that existed beyond the physical world.

Some believed magic and technology were opposing ideas.

The Old Civilisation disagreed.

They believed both were simply different methods of understanding the same universe.

Their greatest discoveries came from combining these ideas.

Their greatest mistakes may have come from doing so.

---

# **The Things That Remain**

The wilderness is no longer simply unexplored.

It is unknown.

Deep beneath the earth, strange creatures dwell in places untouched by sunlight. In abandoned forests and forgotten caves, things move that do not behave like anything found in nature.

Some horrors appear to be remnants of the collapse.

Some appear to have arrived afterwards.

Some records suggest they were created by the Old Civilisation.

Others suggest they were never created at all.

The creatures, anomalies, and phenomena found throughout the world are still poorly understood.

Researchers have tried to classify them.

Mages have tried to communicate with them.

Engineers have tried to study them.

Nobody has found an answer.

Only one thing is certain:

Something changed.

---

# **The Final Journey**

Before the collapse, the Old Civilisation reached further than ever before.

They built machines capable of reaching beyond their world. They developed new methods of travel, new sources of power, and new ways to survive hostile environments.

They were not simply exploring.

They were searching.

The final records speak of a great departure.

Ships were prepared.

Experiments were completed.

A mission was launched.

The Old Civilisation attempted to leave behind whatever had overtaken their world.

But the records end before the truth is known.

Did they escape?

Did they fail?

Or did they discover that there was nowhere left to run?

Now, centuries later, the descendants of humanity have inherited their machines, their magic, and their mistakes.

The ruins remain.

The signals continue.

The darkness waits.

And somewhere among the forgotten remnants of the Old Civilisation lies the answer to the question that started everything:

**What happened after The Correction?**
