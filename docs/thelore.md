# The Lore of the Modpack — Finalised Version

## The Core Premise

This is a world after an apocalypse.

But not a normal apocalypse.

There was no great war, no meteor, no simple collapse.

The world ended because humanity discovered something it was never meant to understand.

The survivors call the people who came before them **the Old Civilisation**.

That is not their true name.

Nobody knows their true name anymore.

Different groups have their own interpretations:

* historians call them **the Old Civilisation**
* engineers call them **the Previous Age**
* mystics call them **the Fallen**
* settlers simply call them **the people before**

Every name says more about the person using it than the civilisation itself.

The truth has been fragmented.

---

# The World Before

The Old Civilisation was not just advanced.

It was impossibly advanced.

They mastered multiple fields simultaneously:

* mechanical engineering
* industrialisation
* automation
* artificial intelligence and computation
* advanced energy systems
* space travel
* magical research
* dimensional studies

The important detail:

They were not divided into "science versus magic."

To them, these were simply different ways of understanding reality.

Some people studied machines.

Some studied energy.

Some studied magic.

Some studied things nobody should have studied.

---

The remnants of their civilisation are everywhere:

* abandoned industrial facilities
* forgotten research sites
* ruined settlements
* ancient magical locations
* underground complexes
* broken networks
* structures whose purpose has been lost

The player does not discover a dead world.

They discover a world that was **abandoned**.

---

# The Incident

Nobody agrees on what happened.

There is no official name.

Different surviving records refer to it as:

* The Incident
* The Breach
* The Event
* The Opening
* The Beginning

The reason there is no agreed history is because the Old Civilisation itself erased it.

---

The event happened when humanity crossed a boundary.

The exact cause is unknown.

And that ambiguity is intentional.

Different disciplines have different explanations.

---

## The Engineer's Explanation

The engineers believe:

> "We built something we did not understand."

Perhaps a machine.
Perhaps an energy experiment.
Perhaps a failed attempt at manipulating reality.

To them, the disaster was a technological accident.

---

## The Mage's Explanation

The arcane scholars believe:

> "We reached beyond our limits."

Perhaps humanity touched something through magic.

Perhaps they opened a door.

Perhaps they called something that was already listening.

---

## The Archivist's Explanation

The researchers studying old data believe:

> "The truth was deliberately hidden."

The records were not simply destroyed.

They were edited.

Removed.

Purged.

Someone wanted future generations to never know.

---

## The Explorer's Explanation

Those who study the ruins believe:

> "This has happened before."

Humanity was not the first civilisation to encounter whatever came next.

They may not even have been the first civilisation to fall.

---

# The Correction

The greatest revelation of the entire story:

The thing that destroyed humanity was not an enemy.

It was not a monster.

It was not a god.

It was **The Correction**.

---

The Correction is something beyond normal understanding.

It is not a creature with thoughts, emotions, or intentions.

It does not hate humanity.

It does not want revenge.

It does not even recognise humanity as important.

---

The best comparison:

If reality is a system, then The Correction is a repair process.

The Old Civilisation created an error.

Something that should not exist entered reality.

The Correction began removing the error.

---

Humanity believed:

> "Something is attacking us."

The truth:

> "Something is fixing the universe."

---

# The Horrors

The creatures and phenomena encountered in the world are not one organised force.

They are symptoms.

They are consequences.

They are what happens when reality is damaged.

---

## The Observers

Things that became aware of humanity.

They watch.

They follow.

They appear.

They do not behave like predators.

Examples:

* The Man From The Fog
* From The Fog

Their question:

> "Why are they looking at us?"

---

## The Imitations

Things attempting to understand humanity.

They copy.

They reproduce patterns.

But they do not understand what they are copying.

Example:

* The Mimicer

Their question:

> "What happens when something learns humanity incorrectly?"

---

## The Residuals

Places where reality never fully recovered.

Examples:

* haunted locations
* strange structures
* abandoned areas

Their question:

> "What remains after something has been removed?"

---

## The Altered

Life changed by exposure.

Creatures, environments, and ecosystems that adapted to a broken world.

Their question:

> "What does life become when reality itself changes?"

---

# The Great Purge

Eventually, the Old Civilisation understood something.

They could not win.

They could not defeat The Correction.

They could only survive.

---

They began destroying their own research.

Not because they regretted discovering things.

Because they understood the danger of repeating the process.

---

They hid:

* technology
* magical knowledge
* research
* archives
* warnings

They intentionally created a broken history.

A civilisation with incomplete memories.

---

The survivors inherited myths.

Because the truth was considered more dangerous than ignorance.

---

# The HS-LGTFO Mission

The final act of the Old Civilisation was an escape attempt.

The official project name has been lost.

Only fragments remain.

The surviving translation:

**HS-LGTFO**

The name itself suggests something unofficial.

Something created by desperate engineers rather than governments.

---

The purpose:

Escape.

Not conquer.

Not fight.

Leave.

---

The mission had three goals:

## 1. Preserve Humanity

Save:

* genetic records
* knowledge
* populations
* technology

---

## 2. Find Somewhere Safe

They built spacecraft.

They searched other worlds.

They looked beyond Earth.

This is where the Ad Astra progression comes in.

---

## 3. Preserve The Truth

They knew they might fail.

So they tried to leave enough information behind.

---

# The Great Mystery

The player eventually discovers:

The HS-LGTFO Mission worked.

---

Humanity did escape.

At least partially.

---

But they did not escape The Correction.

---

The Old Civilisation fled Earth thinking:

> "We need to find somewhere untouched."

They eventually discovered:

> "There was nowhere untouched."

---

The universe itself was affected.

Earth was not the battlefield.

Earth was the first place humanity noticed the war.

---

# The Player's Role

The player is not a chosen hero.

They are a descendant.

Someone who inherited:

* broken machines
* incomplete records
* abandoned cities
* forgotten warnings

---

At first, the player believes:

> "The world ended."

Then:

> "The Old Civilisation failed."

Then:

> "They knew something we don't."

Finally:

> "They were right."

---

# The Four Paths of Discovery

The player can approach the mystery from different directions.

---

# Engineering

The player rebuilds the machines.

They ask:

> "What did humanity create?"

They eventually discover:

Humanity had the power to do almost anything.

That was the problem.

---

# Magic

The player studies the unseen.

They ask:

> "What did humanity contact?"

They discover:

The universe contains things humanity was never designed to perceive.

---

# AE2 / Archives

The player recovers information.

They ask:

> "What did humanity hide?"

They discover:

The Old Civilisation knew the truth.

They chose silence.

---

# Horror Investigation

The player observes what remains.

They ask:

> "What survived?"

They discover:

The horrors are not invading.

They are evidence.

---

# The Final Convergence

All paths eventually lead to the same question.

Not:

"How do we defeat The Correction?"

That is impossible.

---

The question is:

> "Do we leave?"

---

The player rebuilds everything the Old Civilisation once had:

* industry
* magic
* automation
* knowledge
* civilisation
* space travel

They unknowingly recreate the same path.

---

# The Final Truth

The Old Civilisation did not disappear because they were weak.

They did not lose a war.

They did not fail.

They succeeded.

They escaped.

---

But they discovered the terrible truth:

They were not running from a disaster.

They were running from the nature of reality itself.

---

The final horror is not:

> "There is something out there."

The final horror is:

> "There has always been something out there."

---

And the player must decide:

Do they follow the Old Civilisation?

Do they launch the HS-LGTFO mission again?

Or do they stay on a world that was never truly theirs?

---

The pack's final message:

**Humanity's greatest achievement was discovering the universe.**

**Humanity's greatest mistake was assuming the universe wanted to be discovered.**
